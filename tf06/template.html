Techfest '06

Hello there,

Techfest, Asia's largest science and technology festival, is back with its ninth edition, Techfest '06, to be held from the 20th – 22nd January, 2006.A prominent feature of Techfest has been its dynamism and innovativeness. Techfest '06 has taken a giant step by departing from its previously established events and competitions to come up with six "themes" which focus on hot and happening technologies in various fields.

There are lots of happening events and competitions lined up for you at Techfest '06 including Hover Junior, under Sixth Gear, which is a competition just for school students (up to 10+2)

Read on for more information on the different themes and the events under each theme.

A.I.

The name says it all. Artificial Intelligence - The buzzword of the future. We present you with the opportunity to create and code machines of tomorrow that obey orders, learn through experience and try to emulate humans as closely as possible. Through a series of lectures, workshops and competitions, we shall give you a glimpse of things to come, things that are at the heart of Artificial Intelligence!

Micromouse - Masters of the Maze

In this prestigious event, teams must build an autonomous machine that can navigate a maze and reach the destination in the fastest time possible. The mouse must be entirely self-contained and autonomous. Use of computers for controlling the machine is not allowed. Also no information regarding the maze can be fed to the mouse once the maze is revealed. Last date for registration of entries is 10th December 2005. Techfest ’05 saw the maze conquered in a blistering 17 seconds – an Indian record. Do you have it in you to better this time and be the Master of the Maze???

Character Recognition

Participants must code a program which can identify mouse-drawn/stylus-written on-screen characters. The input characters will be in the Devanagari script and the output must be displayed on screen in a console in UNICODE format. The use of MATLAB/MATHEMATICA/SCILAB or any environment similar to these is strictly prohibited but teams can use programming languages like C/C++/VB/Fortran etc. As a screening procedure, all teams have to submit an abstract regarding the algorithm and flowchart of the code. Selected entries will be called to IIT Bombay where the competition will be held in two stages. In the first stage, teams will be asked to identify characters and in the second stage, they have to make a presentation to the judges about their code, algorithm etc.

Last date for registration as well as abstract submission is 10th December, 2005.

Core War

Techfest ’06 presents the battle of programs – Core War. Build a program whose objective is to terminate (kill) all processes of the opposing program leaving your program in charge of the machine. The programs must be written in the assembly language Redcode and will be run on a virtual computer called MARS. The hill (environment) is the battlefield for the programs where they will combat with other warriors (programs). A challenging warrior makes it to the hill by displacing a warrior on the hill with a lower score than the challenger. In a series of battles, only one warrior will emerge victorious and stay on the hill to be crowned the ‘King of the Hill’. Remember, only the fittest shall survive.

Grey Circuits

This workshop introduces you to the exciting areas of Artificial Intelligence like Artificial Neural Networks, Fuzzy Logic etc. and explains its applications in various branches of engineering as well as implementation using MATLAB. Some elementary knowledge of MATLAB is a prerequisite. The workshop consists of 4 lecture sessions spread over the 3 days of the festival and most importantly 2 hands on sessions that will follow.

Registrations close on 20th December 2005, 11:59 PM IST.

Crux

Do you have an idea which you would like to showcase? Crux is the technical paper presentation competition at Techfest ’06 where in a participant can choose any topic related to the field of artificial intelligence and its branches like neural networks, natural language processing etc. and write a paper on the same. The entries that pass the elimination round will then be called to make a technical presentation during Techfest '06.

Last date for registrations and submission of paper is 10th December, 2005.



Concreate

Architecture and construction have always been synonymous with progress and advancement. From the Pyramids of Egypt to the Petronas Towers in Malaysia, man has come a long way. The future holds more potential than we can possibly imagine - be it in terms of materials or design. At CONCREaTE, we give you a platform to display your skills in building futuristic structures with a variety of materials.

H

This competition involves designing and building a helipad using only Popsicle (ice cream) sticks, threads and standard adhesives. Teams must build a flat, horizontal and continuous surface, with provision for clamping onto 4 columns 25 cm below this surface. The lightest platform that can stand the maximum load of 30 kg on the flat deck (helipad) for a minimum of 30 seconds will be declared the winner.

Last date for registration of entries is 31st December, 2005.

Bridged

Try your hand at building a bridge, which satisfies certain criteria, in a professional manner. The bridge must be plane truss in nature and designed for a load capacity of 800 N/m with a safety factor of 1.5. The deck of the bridge must 60 x 250 cm in dimension. Entries are to be made in the form of sealed tenders which will include the detailed hand drawn drawings of the various sections of the bridge, the material as well as labor cost of construction (refer site for more details), time for construction and the deflection that the bridge will undergo under load. These tenders have to be submitted to the organizers and short listed teams will be given a chance during Techfest '06 to make a PowerPoint presentation detailing their design. Entries selected from the presentation will then be given a chance to actually build the structure with the specified materials in the time specified in their tenders.

Last date for registration is 15th December 2005 and for submitting tenders is 31st December 2005.



Cosmos

Humans, for generations, have been intrigued by the eternity we call our Universe, yet we seem to know very little about the happenings beyond the horizons of our Solar System. Cosmos is the theme of space exploration at Techfest '06. Through a series of competitions, lectures, workshops and mind boggling displays, we aim to showcase some of the most breathtaking expeditions to space, their impact on mankind and bring you up to date on the story so far.

G.R.I.P.

The International Space Station's fuel cell from its power chamber has been dislodged due to a meteor collision and the ISS is on the verge of collapsing unless something is done fast…

Can you save the day?

Participants must devise a Galactic Recovery Instrumental Probe (G.R.I.P.) – a wired/wireless remote controlled machine that can traverse a grid made of cylindrical pipes, recover a cylindrical object (fuel cell) from a specific point below the grid and place it in a given slot.

The competition has a video elimination round and the teams must mail a parcel with an abstract of the machine and a CD/DVD showing their machine in action (refer website for details). Selected entries will then be notified and the finals will be held at IIT Bombay.

Last date for registration as well as receipt of parcels is 20th December, 2005.

ASU's Mars Rover Exhibition

The Arizona State University (ASU), USA has been collaborating with NASA on the Mars Rover Project. In May 2005, ASU was invited to the China Science & Technology Week to display the technologies in use. Needless to mention, it was a big hit and over a million people attended the ASU exhibition!

A similar exhibition will be held at Techfest this year. India is only the second country outside the US where this exhibition is being held!!! ASU will be sending a team of 4-5 NASA researchers to guide us through the exhibition. Some highlights of what can be expected:

i) Display of scaled-down models of the space-craft, the rovers and probes on Mars, and full-sized models of the instruments fitted onto the probes and space-craft. Also on display will be fist-sized meteorites collected by ASU over the years.

ii) Latest videos, fresh from the rovers on Mars, to be beamed down at this exhibition!!!

iii) Video-clippings of the highlights of the mission, right from launch of the space-craft to touchdown on Mars, from panoramic views captured by the rovers on Mars to flight-views of the canyons of Mars!!! And lots more!!

Black Box

Solar energy is the most easily accessible form of energy in space and solar sails are used to harness this energy. A solar sail is a large, flat surface that can be used to harness the energy of the sun to propel a spacecraft around the solar system. Teams must build a solar sail that will initially fit into a box of given dimensions. On receiving a single electronic/mechanical impulse it should be able to expand to have the maximum projected area in the given direction. The sail can have any shape and must not be made of any shiny or reflecting material.

The last date for registration is 20th December, 2005.

Mission Moon 2030

What do you think would take to build a human settlement on the moon? Write a detailed step by step report on how you would establish a large scale, permanent and self sustained human settlement on the moon. This might just turn out to be your chance to get into the history books!

The last date for registration is 25th December, 2005. This is an online competition and strictly individual affair. The papers should be mailed at missionmoon@techfest.org latest by 10th January, 2006 11:59 PM, with the subject as your registration number.

ISRO Exhibition: "India in Space"

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has launched project ‘Chandrayan Pratham’ – a mission to explore the moon. This project also involves sending an unmanned lunar probe before the year 2008.

Highlights of the exhibition

1. Models of previous Space Vehicles launched by ISRO.

2. Story boards about India’s Moon Mission Chandrayan.

3. Interactive sessions with Scientists of ISRO, the people in lead of India’s Moon Mission – Chandrayan.



e-Rustique

e–Rustique is an attempt by Techfest to take a major step forward in rural technology by showing how technology can be used to tackle some of the core issues faced in villages like communications, infrastructure, health and education. We intend to engage the student community in this noble endeavor and believe that Techfest would indeed be an ideal platform for the youth to come up with novel solutions to existing problems.

The Simple Life v1.1

Develop a software that can be used in the village kiosks after analyzing the situation – inherent advantages, handicaps, abilities, weaknesses, etc. - of the village and the villagers and take appropriate action. Certain data like the population, occupations, physical conditions etc will be provided, based on which the analysis has to be made.

The 'project' will undergo development to completion by interaction with the industry and will comprise of two main stages. The first stage is the calling for ideas, wherein a shortlist will be made, and the second will be the actual development period with assistance provided by industry.

On the basis of abstracts submitted, a list of selected entries along with their synopsis has been displayed on our website. If you have an idea that could play an important role in a certain project and the team has less than four members (max. team size) then with the permission of the short listed team you could join them in their project. Team expansion is entirely at the discretion of the selected teams and not compulsory for all teams. The last date for team expansion is 20th December, 2005.

Innovosion

Rural India is faced by a number of problems that can be solved by incorporating simple cost-effective technology. At Innovision, participants must come up with solutions to problems relevant to the current rural scenario in India. The solution may be innovative or an improvised version of an already existing solution. Teams must submit an abstract of their ideas detailing the relevance of the problem, the extent of implementation possible and cost effectiveness. A list of some relevant problems is provided on our website. Selected teams will then be called to IIT Bombay during Techfest '06 to display a working solution/prototype/model.

Entries for Innovision can also take part in the Anveshan Innovator competition at IIM Ahmedabad. If selected you get a chance to float your company!!! Also Innovision gives you a direct entry into another of our competitions - Technovision (see Technoholix for details)

The last date for registration as well as submission of abstracts is 10th December 2005.

Agrobot

Build a wired remote controlled machine which can dig holes in mud and plant saplings. The machine(s) must have the capacity to carry 15 saplings that will be pre loaded on it. It must then plant ten of these saplings in ten pits on the arena in a maximum time of 10 minutes. The extra element of fun is provided by pitting two teams against each other and the team which finishes the task fastest with minimum penalties is declared the winner.

The last date for registration is 20th December, 2005.

ICT Exhibition

Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) play a very important role in our lives today. At Techfest ’06 you will get a chance to know more about ICT through the ICT exhibition conducted by Media Labs, Asia. On display will be some major applications of ICT. Mr. Vipul Arora of Stanford University will be helping us with the details and under his guidance a setup of eMandi will be created to showcase and elaborate on ICT.



Sixth Gear

The wheel is probably one of the greatest inventions of mankind. Cars and planes have dramatically changed our lives, giving a whole new meaning to travel. Man has been obsessed with speed for many years now and even today, keeps striving for that extra bit of speed, that extra degree of efficiency. Every day heralds the start of something new in the automobile and aviation sector.

At Sixth Gear we give you the opportunity to get back behind the wheels and drive through the latest and best in auto-tech and aviation technology and try a hands-on approach while learning the nuances of some of the most complicated machines around.

Full Throttle

Make a remote controlled car that can cover a given stretch of a flat and straight track in the shortest possible time. The machine can be powered by up to 3.2 cc IC engines. The participants must also devise their own starting mechanisms like a launching pad as well a stopping mechanism for the engine to stop running after the finish line. The machine must have a remote control mechanism to drive the car.

The last date for registration is 10th December, 2005.

Hover Junior

Junior is the competition at Techfest for students up to 10+2. For Hover, participants must build a hovercraft that travels along a given path and accomplish given tasks. The hovercraft has to descend along a 5-degree incline, cross a pit and then cross a water-body and finally knock down three loosely fixed flags before turning back and coming to starting point. All this will be in competition with a rival hovercraft and must be done of-course with an air-cushion at all times between the craft and the surface below.

Worskshops - roboTRIx

roboTRIx is a workshop designed by TRI (The Robotics Institute - a venture founded by a group of IIT Bombay alumnus) for newbies and robotics enthusiasts of all disciplines. The aim of the workshop is to increase the profile of robotics learning. Participants will be introduced to the basic fundamentals of robotics, their applications as well as designing a robot from scratch. Students will have to register as a team of maximum three students. Registration charges for each team will be Rs 3000/-. These charges cover the cost incurred for TRI Robotic Kit which will be given to the team (one kit per team). There will be limited entries for this workshop and teams will be selected on the basis of a Statement of Purpose (SOP) that must be submitted before 20th December which is also the last date for registration.

Future FLight

Dig deep into your brains and come up with innovative ideas to design flying cars. Use the platform provided by Techfest '06 to launch your lofty ideas. Supplement your ideas with scientific feasibility; discussing the practicality of the solution sought and explain in detail the working of your idea.

The last date for registration is 25th December, 2005. This is an online competition and strictly individual affair. The papers should be mailed at future_flight@techfest.org latest by 10th January, 2006 11:59 PM, with the subject as your registration number.

Workshops - Iterations

The main objective of Iterations is a detailed look at Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) on different fronts including the concepts involved as well as CFD software. There will also be competitions to find solutions to fluid mechanics problem using the CFD software and prizes will be awarded to the winners of these competitions.

The last date for registration is 16th December. Only limited entries will be selected on the basis of a questionnaire that will be mailed to all registered participants.

FlyHigh

Make a wireless remote controlled flying machine that flies along the air-path provided, passes through three rectangular rings and returns to the starting point. The machine has to be necessarily controlled by a wireless control mechanism throughout the competition. The last date for registration is 20th December, 2005.



Smart Tech

Technology today is an integral part of our lives with a wide variety of gadgets and gizmos lying at our disposal. In modern times, it has made life seem like a cakewalk, what with the lavish comforts and luxuries it gives us, and dispensing of our necessities with further efficiency. However this is just the beginning and the future lies in wait promising much, much more!

Smart Tech gets you up-close to gadgets and systems which were a distant dream in the recent past and are still unknown to many. Bringing the latest in this vibrant, new dimension to technology, we make sure you see what might be prevalent in our lives in the decades to come.

iClean

Teams must build an autonomous machine that can collect a specific amount of material-puffed rice (murmure) from the board and dump it into a specified zone of the arena. The machine must have an onboard mechanism to measure the weight of the contents collected. The machine is required to collect the puffed rice randomly scattered in its zone, get past obstacles on the playing field and dump the contents in the pit. The machine may transit multiple times to dump the required load.

Last date for registration is 10th December, 2005.

Prayaas

At Techfest '05 Drishti, a competition to make aids for the visually impaired was launched. Taking off from where we left, Techfest '06, in association with the National Association for the Blind (NAB) and the Happy Hours Centre (HHC), presents Prayaas. Prayaas is an attempt to make the lives of the physically disabled simpler and more comfortable. Participants can choose problem statements from the website or one of their own and develop smart workable solutions. The teams must submit an abstract for the screening round outlining their solution. Select teams will be called to Techfest '06 to showcase their idea for the finals.

Entries for Prayaas will also qualify directly as entries for Technovision (see Technoholix for details). Last date for registration and submission of abstracts is 20th December 2005.

Industry Defined Problem

Can you provide a solution to a problem on the practical front as faced by the industry?

Design an eco-friendly, energy efficient desalination device for household use. The best solution attracts an incentive Prize Money of Rs.25, 000 and an exciting opportunity to work as an intern at General Electric's John F. Welch Technology Centre, Bangalore.

Home Alive

How would we define an ideal home? One in which all our requirements are met and all our work is simplified to the maximum possible extent. Home Alive takes a look at the concept of smart homes. The teams have to design a smart home on a given layout, incorporating the systems mentioned and give a presentation highlighting its salient features. The competition will be held in two stages. The first stage, an elimination round, requires teams to write an abstract on smart security solutions for the given layout. The teams that qualify will then have to make a presentation, in IIT Bombay at Techfest '06 outlining other smart systems that can be incorporated for the same layout. The technologies used must be existing technologies.

The last date for registration and submission of abstracts is 10th December, 2005.

Workshop - Codec

Video coding is the field that deals with finding efficient compression formats for digital video (DV). It finds various smart applications, e.g. in Videoconferencing and Video telephony, (VOIP), telemedicine, wireless/mobile applications, distance education etc. The workshop will start from the basics of Video Coding and get into the details of video encoder and decoder structure. It will also cover various existing compression standards in Video Coding. As a minimum requirement the participants need to have some basic coding skills and elementary knowledge of MATLAB.

Registrations close on 24th December 2005, 11:59 PM IST.



Technoholix
SciTech: College Quiz Challenge

SciTech is for all you technology and quizzing enthusiasts and brings to the fore some of the best minds of the country. If you have a sharp and alert mind, with a decent knowledge of science and technology then SciTech is the place for you to be. The culminating event of Techfest, the SciTech final is a grand event conducted with latest technologies in the open air theatre of IIT Bombay in front of a crowd of over 5000 people.

Technovision Awards

Every year at Techfest we see a number of brilliant ideas surfacing in different competitions. Some of them win prizes while some others go largely unnoticed but a vast majority of ideas never go beyond the competition level and gradually fade away into oblivion.

To catch brilliant and innovative ideas and tap their potential, Techfest ’06 in association with CHIP launches the Technovision awards for innovations in pure sciences and engineering. Become a ‘technovisionary’ at Asia’s largest science and technological festival, get international recognition and take home a number of prizes. Do you have it in you??

Teams must first submit an abstract detailing the main points of their idea. Selected teams will be called to make a presentation of their idea before a panel of judges at Techfest ’06. Participation in Prayaas and Innovision will give teams a direct entry into Innovision and no separate abstracts have to be submitted by these teams.

Last date for registration and abstract submission is December 20th, 2005.
