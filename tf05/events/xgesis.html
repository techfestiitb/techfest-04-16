Xgesis - The Technical Exposition

To start with, what is an exposition? Is it a statement, an answer, an explanation? Maybe. But whatever it is, you need to know what you speak.

Techfest presents Xgesis the technical exposition. Take up any topic of your choice, absolutely any topic... related to science and technology and then the rest is all very simple. Just tell us what you have to say on the topic and walk away with a grand prize. You may encounter a bit of competition from other aspirants, but then, what's a reward without a struggle, right?

So here are the rules for the arena
No whistling
No jeering
No cat-calls
(Oops! sorry wrong competition)

Topic

The topic can be anything related to science and technology.

Eligibilty

All students with a valid identity card of their respective institute are eligible to participate in Xgesis at Techfest 2005

Competition structure

  1. This competition is a strictly individual affair. For the first round of elimination, send in a detailed abstract of your topic latest by 30th November 2004.The abstract must give a complete overview of your topic, highlighting its salient points and must not exceed three page and must be submitted as a MS-word document in size 10 Arial font.The abstract should be mailed at xgesis@techfest.org with the subject as your registration number. The Ms-word document should be attached to this mail.
  2. No entries will be entertained after the date mentioned above.
  3. A few select entries will be called to IIT Bombay where they will go through a second round of elimination in which they will have to make a 15 minute presentation on their topic, on the basis of which, they will be selected for the final round to be held from 28th - 30th January 2005.

Props.

The participants can use audio and visual aids for the exposition. We will provide you with a blackboard, a computer with MS- Office, an LCD projector and/or an OHP.

Duration

Final Round.
The presentation/explanation must not exceed 30 minutes under any circumstance, and points will be deducted for exceeding the time limit. This will be followed by a brief cross examination by the judges, for a maximum of 10 minutes.

Judging

Marks will be awarded on the basis of the following schema:-

  1. Content
    • Relevance of presentation to the chosen topic.
    • Clarity of concepts and response to questions by judges.
  2. Presentation
    • Effectiveness of visual aids - They must not substitute your talk, just complement it.
    • Communication (NOT "language skills") - There must be continuity in the building up of the topic.

A few tips

  • Keep your presentation within the time limit; a long presentation does not go well.
  • Novelty in the topic helps in rousing the judges' interest.
  • You can Google but so can we!
