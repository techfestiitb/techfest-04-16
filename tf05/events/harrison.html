Dr. Andrew Harrison

Dr. Andrew Harrison is currently the Professor of Solid State Chemistry at the University of Edinburgh. After completing his B.A., M.A. and D. Phil from the University of Oxford, he joined Oxford as a Junior Fellow. Since then he has held positions as a Research Fellow at the atomic energy of Canada Ltd., Visiting Professor, RIKEN, Tokyo and currently he is also Deputy Head of Planning Unit, Chemistry, Edinburgh, Director of Centre for Science at Extreme Conditions, The University of Edinburgh.

His research interests include:

Microwave methods to new materials using hydrothermal synthesis, plasma technology and solid-state routes.

Design, synthesis and study of functional materials, and in particular magnets and phosphors for fundamental and applied science.

X-ray and neutron diffraction studies of magnetic structures and excitations, and in situ chemical processes.
