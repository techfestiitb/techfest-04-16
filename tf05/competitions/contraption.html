Contraption

Make a glass of Lemonade ("Nimbu Pani").

There are three essential ingredients ( viz. water, sugar, lemon ) and one essential process (viz. stirring.) Your machine should make a glass of lemon juice ( at least 100 ml). All you have to do is to perform this trivial task in most complex and intriguing manner, maximising number of significant steps ( refer rules). You may use the lemon in any suitable form.

Total cash prize of Rs 18,000 to be won!

Rules & Regulations

  • Students currently enrolled for up to an undergraduate/postgraduate program at their institute are eligible for the competition.
  • Number of members per team should not exceed three.
  • Every team has to register online at our website for the competition. A registration number will be allocated to the team on registration which shall be used for future reference.
  • Any of the length, breadth or height of the machine, when set up, should not exceed 2 meter.
  • The machine must complete the task as described in the problem statement.
  • The machine must have a minimum of eight significant steps. There is no limit to the maximum number of steps. ( A significant step is defined as a step or a series of steps, which involves a net displacement of essential ingredients or the stirring of the mixture.) Steps, constructed using the same mechanism will be counted only once.
  • There should be a minimum of five different energy transformations in the contraption.
  • No human intervention is allowed once the machine starts. A maximum of three trials will be given to each machine to perform.
  • Teams will be given only one hour prior to the competition to set up their contraption at the venue.
  • Each team must submit a concise copy of a step-by-step description of their machine to the competition coordinator as soon as it starts setting up the machine at the contest venue.
  • The participants are encouraged to bring a chart showing the step-by-step working of the contraption for the convenience of the spectators.
  • Judges' decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.
  • The last date for registration is 5th Jan 2005.

Safety and General Norms

  • No hazardous materials or explosives can be used.
  • The machine must be safe to the satisfaction of the judges.
  • Each team should remove its machine and clean up its area immediately after the contest.
  • A 230 V/50 Hz AC power supply will be made available at the venue.
