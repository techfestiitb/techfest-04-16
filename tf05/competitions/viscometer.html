Viscometer

The notion of viscosity was first introduced by Newton as a constant relating shear rate and shear stress in the mid-17th Centruy. Viscosity can be vaguely defined as the resistance to fluid motion. Viscosity is one of the fundamental properties of matter just as density, heat capacity etc.

The importance of viscosity can be appriciated through the Navier-Stokes equation. This basic equation governs all the simple as well as Newtonian fluid dynamics on all scales ranging from the cellular systems to reaction vessles. The value of viscosity of the fluid mplays a major role in determining the flow pattern under given conditions.

Thus it is a practical and significant exercise to measure the viscosity of fluids. Finding applications in almost all forms of engineernig.

Chemsplash at Techfest 2005 brings to you <> the Viscometer design competition, where you'll be judged for creativity and accuracy in a design cum "hands on" competition.

Total cash prize of Rs 13,000 to be won!

Problem Statement

Design and craft a viscometer which will measure the viscosities of 3 different fluids within the range of 1-600 cP.

Competition Rules

  • The team can consist of a maximum of 2 students.
  • The participants will have to submit a design abstract which would include the theoretical calculations and a rough error estimate analysis on 28th of January, 2005 before 5 pm by email, to chemsplash@techfest.org.
  • The entries will be tested on 3 different liquids for viscosity measurements. These measurements will take place during Techfest 2005 (29th or 30th January, 2005) at IIT Bombay.
  • A maximum of 200 ml of each kind of liquid will be provided to participants. The readings are to be taken using amount mentioned. Requests fot additional liquid shall not be entertained. All the liquids provided will be Newtonian.
  • The faculty can only act as a guidance through design and crafting of the viscometer, they shouldn't be an active part of the team.

Judging criterion

  • Design abstract (includes creativity, implemenatability, error analysis) - 40%
  • Agreement of the practical and theoretical viscosity values and error - 40%
  • Judges' questions - 20%

Note: All the fluids provided will be Newtonian.
