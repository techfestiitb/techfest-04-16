Survivor FAQ

What are the specifications of the dimensions of the machine?

The machine has to fit in a box of 25*25*25 cm at any point of time.

Can I use Parachute to land on the ground?

Parachutes can be used for landing given the condition the parachute comes With in the dimensions of the machine.

Can I use a compressor from outside as a power source?

The machine must use only mechanical power or mechanical power converted from a source of electrical energy. Chemicals, compressed gas and rockets, fuels, combustion power methods are strictly prohibited.

Can I break up my machine into two parts at any point in the game?

Breaking any part or leaving any part on the arena is not allowed n can lead to Disqualification

Will there be any extra credit for a complete autonomous machine?

No, the whole game is based on the point system and no extra points are awarded for a completely autonomous machine.

After the machine comes to a standstill on Stage 3a.Can a team member align the machine by moving it?

Yes, the machine can be adjusted to be at a right spot before the start of the line following stage

What are the specifications of the carpet?

This horizontal portion is covered with compact coir mat of thickness 3 cm and density 80 mg/cc. The mat is covered with a Herringbone type woven coir rug of thickness 1 cm.

In Stage 2b what is the maximum number of blocks a machine can put in their zone or the holders?

The Stage 2b is an open round and both the machines can attempt as many blocks as possible

Entering into the red zone can lead to what kind of penalty?

Penalty for entering the red zone in Stage 1b is - 20 points.

Is there any criss-crossing of the white curved line in Stage 3?

No

What are the rule sfor the video elimination

For the video eliminations, the teams must send a parcel (e submissions acceptable on special request) containing the following to the address mentioned below. Contents of the parcel to be sent:

  1. An abstract of not more than two A4 sheets covering essentially the principle used by the machine, the approximate time taken to complete the task and the projected final cost of the machine.
  2. A Compact Disc (C.D.) containing the video of the machine in action. It should essentially contain the following:
    • A portion of this tape must be an uninterrupted and unedited recording showing the machine jumping off a 1 m high platform onto a floor with 4 cm thick coir flooring. Then the machine has to travel up to a point (turn point), marked at a distance of 2 m parallel to the length of the platform, from the foot of the platform, starting from where it lands, take a U-turn and travel back to the foot of the platform. The machine should be radio controlled all this time. A video timer must be visible during this uninterrupted recording. There should be a measuring tape placed along the height of the platform and also from the foot of the platform to the turn point.
    • A detailed look at and explanation of the machine, including close-up shots and shots from various directions.
    • Participants are essentially required to have a narration (in English) also along with the video.

Whether readymade kits can be used in the machine?

The machine should not be made using Lego parts, assemblies from toys etc. However the remote control systems from toys can be used. However, standard kits like the Basic Stamp kits may be used.
