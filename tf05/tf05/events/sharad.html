Mr. Sharad Sharma

Sharad Sharma is currently the General Manager, India Operations and Vice President, Product Operations, for Veritas Software Inc.

Before joining Veritas, Sharad was the co-founder and CEO of Teltier Technologies Inc., a New Jersey-based telecom start-up focusing on products for the mobile network operators in Europe, and driving the standards in the area of Presence and Availability Management.

Incorporated in 2001, Teltier was funded by St. Paul VC, a Boston-based fund, as the lead investor, and Cir Labs of Italy and the NJ Technology Council as supporting VCs. He successfully sold the company in the end of 2003 to a larger NJ-based communications company, after which he chose to relocate back to India.

Sharad has over 17 years of experience in the IT / Telecom Industries. His most recent assignment prior to Teltier was in the area of 3G Network Services and Solutions for Lucent Technologies in Whippany, NJ. Prior to this, he was leading the Development for a ‘Wireless in Local Loop’ [WLL] product for Lucent in Ohio.

He was also instrumental in setting up Lucent’s Product Realization Centers in India, and was the first Development Center Head of the Lucent-owned Development Center in India, Bangalore. Sharad was also an early stage employee in a start-up supported by Apple Computer Inc. that was responsible for making shrink-wrap software for the Macintosh.

He started his career with DCM Data Products in their software R&D group in New Delhi where he was working on products in the area of Networking and Communications.

He has been a Member of the IEEE Computer Society and IEEE Technical Committee on Software Engineering. He has spoken at several Technical Seminars and has published papers in such prestigious publications as the Bell Labs Technical Journal. He is an Electrical Engineer from the Delhi College of Engineering. He is passionate about Technology and is an avid reader.

Sharad, has both the ‘big company’ experience and has been a successful entrepreneur. Sharad is passionate about seeing more and more product development happen in India – not just in start-ups but also in the captive and outsourced product development centers.
