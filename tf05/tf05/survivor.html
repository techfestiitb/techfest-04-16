Survivor - The International Challenge

Excerpts from a top secret Moon Mission :

Astronaut : Sir, I don't think She's going to survive the fall.
Ground Control : You do your job and we'll do ours. That's what She was designed for and She won't fail.

Astronaut : It's my life on the line. And even if She does survive....
Ground Control : There's no if. She was made for this fall. Every component is ready and designed with the impact in mind.

Astronaut : But what about later? Will She still be in a healthy enough state to pick up rocks and move them around once she lands?
Ground Control : Learn to trust the designers Son. She will land. She will survive. She will play with the rocks. She will survive. She will find her way there all by herself. And at the end of it all, she will still be alive. Why do you think she's called "Survivor"?

Techfest 2005 proudly brings to you Survivor, the international challenge.

Entries for Survivor are invited from any student from across the world. The participants would be classified into 2 categories - India and Rest of the World for the qualifying round. The teams clearing this round shall attend the finals to be held in Mumbai, India during Techfest 2005 (28th - 30th January, 2005). A few selected qualifying teams from the Rest of the World category will also receive to and fro airfare for the finals. The winning teams from both the categories combined would receive cash prizes of a total of US $ 2500. Please see the links below for complete details of the contest.

Last Date for collection of prize money is 20th March 2005

Elimination Round

The Survivor's second elimination round is scheduled to be held on 26th and 27th of January.

The teams of the first shortlist are going to compete on 27th of Jan, 8 am onwards. The teams of the second shortlist are going to compete on 26th of Jan, 8 am onwards.

In this second elimination round :-

The machine will have to travels up a slope to reach a platform 1 m high then jump off the platform to reach the ground. In Stage 2a of the arena, 3 blocks will be placed at a distance of 30 cm from the centre line, on one side of the arena. The first block is positioned at 40 cm from the gate and the following two at another 50 cm each from the previous one. The machine can keep the blocks either in the holders or in their zone. Then the machine will have to follow a line in an autonomous way. The points for every part remain the same as it is for the final event(http://localhost/www/rules.html#points). The machine will be ranked on the basis of number of points earned within a time span of 10 minutes. Every team will be provided with just one chance to perform on the arena. Incase of a tie, the machine completing the task in the least time will be ranked higher.

Any team violating any of the rules will be eliminated immediately and no requests will be considered later on. The eliminations are going to be very strict.

Top 32 teams out of the ones featuring on 26th and 27th of January collectively will qualify for the final round on 29th January. Teams contesting on 27th January will be the top 41 teams shortlisted earlier.

Problem Statement

Make a wireless remote controlled machine, which travels up a slope to reach a platform 1 m high; then jumps off the platform to reach the ground; completes a task involving some blocks; and then follows a line in an autonomous fashion. The machine fetching itself more number of points at the end of the match is declared as the winner.
