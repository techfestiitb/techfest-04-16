Technology for All

IIT Bombay's Techfest may come off as just another science and technology festival to some, but those who have experienced it would realise just how grossly under-rated that statement sounds. The annual fest held in January has the clear-cut distinction of being the largest fest of its kind in the entirety of Asia. Having gone international from 2004, it, now, strives to be reckoned among the best in the world.

The aim is clear - to initiate and further the spirit of the vibrant and dynamic student community. Techfest achieves this by hosting events with far-reaching impact which interest not only those participating actively in the various competitions but also those people whose only knowledge of cutting-edge technology is limited to everyday gadgets. These events range from robotic competitions, exhibitions of major technological innovations, lectures by eminent personalities dealing with all major aspects of science and technology, workshops providing actual hands-on-experience; to fun-filled contests involving creative concepts like constructing bridges and buildings from drinking-straws!

The latest installment of Techfest, which will be held on 28-30 January 2005, endeavors to bear aloft the torch of the future which has been lit and passed down through seven years of Techfest. The crux of the fest is clear and simple - Technology for All.
