CONTRAPTION
Optimization hinders evolution.

Every scientific development in today’s world is an effort to make things simpler. Ever thought of going the other way? We call it the Dexter’s way.

To burst just a balloon, Dexter opens his lab window, lets sunlight in which activates a solar panel. The power from the panel drives a motor fan which blows the flame of a candle kept near a string. The string burns thus releasing a pin attached to a pendulum which ultimately bursts the balloon placed in the pin’s trajectory.

Whew!
Sensible? … No.
Exciting? … Definitely YES!

All you have to do is to perform a trivial task in most complex and intriguing manner. Go ahead …do something you never did before …make life tougher!

Problem Statement:      To light a bulb


Rules and Regulations:

  1. Students currently enrolled for up to an undergraduate/postgraduate program at their institute are eligible for the competition.

  2. Number of members per team should not exceed four.

  3. Every team has to register online at our website for the competition. A registration number will be allocated to the team on registration which shall be used for future reference.

  4. No dimension of the machine should exceed 6ft.

  5. The machine must complete the task as described in the problem statement.

  6. The machine must have a minimum of twelve steps. There is no limit to the maximum number of steps.

  7. There should be a minimum of four different energy transformations in the contraption.

  8. No human intervention is allowed once the machine starts. A maximum of three trials will be given to each machine to perform.

  9. Teams will be given only one hour prior to the competition to set up their contraption at the venue.

  10. Each team must submit a concise copy of a step-by-step description of their machine to the competition coordinator as soon as it starts setting up the machine at the contest venue.


  11. The participants are encouraged to bring a chart showing the step-by-step working of the contraption for the convenience of the spectators.


  12. Judges decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.


Safety and General Norms:
  1. No hazardous materials or explosives can be used.

  2. The machine must be safe to the satisfaction of the judges.

  3. Each team will remove its machine and clean up its area immediately after the contest.

  4. A 230 V/50 Hz AC power will be made available the venue.
