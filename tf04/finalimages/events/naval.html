NAVY EXHIBITION

First Time Ever On Land
By Indian Navy

The Indian Navy will be actively participating in Techfest 2004. The Western Naval Command of the Indian Navy shall be putting up an exhibition displaying the static and working models as well as actual ship-borne equipment showcasing the cutting edge technology that has made the Indian Navy one of the strongest and most respected forces world wide. The aims of the exhibition would be :-

  • To depict the wide spectrum of technologies used in the Navy.

  • To instill a greater sense of patriotism and national integrity in the youth.

  • To enhance awareness of the youth in particular, to the multifarious activities of one of the most formidable navies in the world.

The theme of the exhibition will be built upon the basic functions of a warship. The exhibits will give an insight into the complex technologies which contribute to making the ship into a cohesive, self- contained, fighting unit. Equipment being displayed would depict the four essential facets of a warship which are :-

  • To Float: What make these HUGE machines of war float? Hull design (all you're authorised to know), dry docking (repair) and more.

  • To Move: Propulsion. Huge propellers (these ships may give a few speedboats a run for their money). Engines the size of a small house.

  • To Seek: Radars, infra red, sensors. The works. Gadgets that serve as the captains eyes in times of war.

  • To Destroy: Guns, missiles, torpedos. This is how the navy takes its enemies down. A rare opportunity to get a glimpse of these weapons.

Every branch of engineering, be it Mechanical, Marine, Metallurgical, Electronics, Electrical, Informatics and many more, plays a significant role in the design, construction and operation of a warship. The exhibition is designed to give a flavour of the varied spectrum of involved technologies, not only in the many fields of Engineering, but also in the evolution of the associated equipment, right from the mid-90s to the present state-of-art.

There will also be photographs, exhibits and banners depicting life in the navy and various extracurricular activities. All in all an excellent opportunity to sneak a glimpse of what the navy is all about.

The exhibition will be inaugurated by Rear Admiral S. K. K. Krishnan, AVSM, VSM, Admiral Superintendent, Naval Dockyard, Mumbai on 24 Jan 2004.
