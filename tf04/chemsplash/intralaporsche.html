Intra la porsChe


The year is 2296 A.D. Earth. After the 4th world war, the world has completely changed. The petroleum based fuels had already been drained in the 3rd world war. The dust holocaust has cut off the sun. All eyes have turned to alternative fuels. Chemicals have been identified as the potential energy source and our aim is to develop a car which can survive the scare. Introducing ' la porsChe'!!!

GAME RULES:

  1. Each team will be asked to travel a specific distance. The distance specified is 15 feet.

  2. The distance traveled by the car will be taken as the distance between the start and the endpoint and the path traveled will not be considered.

  3. The teams will have to be ready with their Car and when their turn comes they will be given a maximum of 2 min. to start it.

  4. The teams will be give TWO chances and the better of the two will be considered for the competition (except in case of a tie).


GENERAL RULES:

  1. The competition will be conducted on the honor system. Faculty can only act as sounding boards to the student queries. The faculty cannot be idea generators for the project.

  2. The maximum team size is 3 participants.

  3. Judges decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.

THE GAME ARENA:

  1. he game arena would be a rectangular area without any form of power supply and adequate safety precautions would be taken in order to avoid any fire hazards in case of some explosion.

  2. In case the Car starts to overflow chemicals it would be obligatory on their part to clean the game arena, else they would be disqualified with immediate effect. Hence participants are expected to carry the required paraphernalia along with them.


THE "CAR":

  1. The car's size should not exceed 30x40x20 cm3 at any time during the game.

  2. Nothing except the chemicals required for the reaction can be adjusted for the Car, after the distance to be traversed is revealed to the team.

  3. Use of any explosion, or reactions causing explosions akin to fire-crackers and rockets is not allowed and would lead to disqualification.

  4. Use of any electronic or mechanical devise for the purpose of distance measurement is not allowed.

  5. No mechanical force can be applied to the wheel or ground or the car to slow or stop the car (e.g. no brakes).
